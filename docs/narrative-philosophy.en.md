# HDS — Narrative and Philosophy: Form and Emptiness, Fact and Mercy

## Form and Emptiness
The hologram is not docs for docs’ sake. It’s the “bones” of the system: boundaries (SURFACE), laws (invariants), canon (determinism/versions), and one living path. Emptiness is room for evolution: the FLUID zone and stubs. Early “flesh” without form creates debt. Early canon without reality cements error. Balance: first form (Surface/invariants), then proof (scenario), then flesh (code).

## Wu Wei (Non‑action) as Engineering Discipline
Avoid changes that trigger chains of rewrites. First Surface/Proof, then code. Don’t fight the nature of tools (LLM): give strict banks (HOLO/SURFACE/tests), not borderless freedom.

## Truth as Reproducible Fact
Project truth is not “feels right” but a reproducible test or counterexample (Refutation). Invariant is the law (intent); test is case law (fact). Together they resist drift.

## Two‑Speed World
Exploration must be fast, but without eroding banks. Frozen/Core changes are slow, backed by facts and migrations. Fluid/Periphery changes are fast but cannot break canon. The boundary is care for the future.

## Mercy to the Future
HOLO and SURFACE are letters to your future self and peers (incl. LLM). They offer bearings: where to look, what to touch, what not to break, how to verify. It’s ethics: fewer surprises, more explainability.

## Mental Recommendations
- Before coding, answer: what changes on the Surface? how will we prove it?
- If an LLM “struggles,” it’s not its fault: it signals cognitive debt. Simplify the form.
- If arguing taste, return to invariants. If none exist, write the law first, then argue tests.
